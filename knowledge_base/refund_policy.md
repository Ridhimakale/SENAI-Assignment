# Refund Policy & Customer Retention Playbook

## Purpose

This document defines refund eligibility, credit issuance guidelines, exception handling procedures, customer retention workflows, and escalation requirements for all customer-facing support teams.

The objective is to ensure consistent treatment of refund requests while minimizing unnecessary customer churn.

---

# 1. Standard Refund Eligibility

Customers may request a refund within 14 calendar days of their initial purchase.

Conditions:

* Account must be in good standing.
* No evidence of abuse or fraudulent activity.
* Refund request must be submitted within the refund window.

Support representatives may approve eligible refunds without managerial review.

---

# 2. Non-Refundable Cases

Refunds are not automatically available when:

* More than 14 days have passed since purchase.
* The customer has substantially consumed the purchased service.
* The account has violated platform policies.
* The request is based solely on future feature expectations.

In these situations, support teams should evaluate alternative retention options before rejecting the request.

---

# 3. Credits vs Refunds

Customer retention should be prioritized whenever reasonable.

Before issuing a refund, support teams should consider:

* Service credits
* Subscription extensions
* Temporary discounts
* Downgrades to lower pricing tiers

Credits are preferred when:

* The customer experienced a minor service disruption.
* The customer remains an active user.
* The customer intends to continue using the platform.

Refunds are preferred when:

* Service delivery failed completely.
* The customer remains within the refund eligibility period.
* A legal or regulatory obligation requires reimbursement.

---

# 4. Exception Process

Refund requests submitted after the standard refund window require exception review.

Exception reviews may be considered when:

* Significant platform outages occurred.
* Billing errors are confirmed.
* Documented service failures caused material business impact.

Exception requests require managerial approval.

Support representatives may not approve exceptions independently.

---

# 5. Enterprise Customer Rules

Enterprise accounts receive additional review before denial of any refund request.

Before denying an enterprise refund request:

* Review account value.
* Review contract renewal status.
* Review active opportunities.
* Review recent support history.

Enterprise exceptions must be documented and escalated to account management.

---

# 6. Churn Retention Playbook

Indicators of churn risk include:

* Cancellation requests.
* Repeated complaints.
* Multiple negative interactions.
* Competitor comparisons.
* Statements indicating intent to leave.

Recommended retention actions:

Low Risk:

* Clarify misunderstandings.
* Offer training resources.

Medium Risk:

* Offer service credits.
* Offer subscription adjustments.

High Risk:

* Escalate to retention team.
* Create internal retention ticket.
* Notify account owner.

Critical Risk:

* Executive review may be required.

---

# 7. Public Review Threat Handling

Customers may threaten:

* Negative Trustpilot reviews.
* Negative G2 reviews.
* Social media escalation.
* Public complaints.

When a customer threatens public reputation damage:

Support teams should:

1. Remain professional.
2. Avoid argumentative responses.
3. Create a retention case.
4. Escalate to customer success leadership when account value is significant.

If the customer has sent multiple unanswered complaints, classify the case as High or Critical churn risk and escalate to customer success leadership.

Public review threats do not automatically qualify a customer for a refund.

However, they should increase the priority of the case.

---

# 8. Escalation Requirements

Escalate immediately when:

* Legal action is threatened.
* Regulatory complaints are mentioned.
* Media inquiries are referenced.
* Enterprise customers indicate contract termination.

Required actions:

* Create internal ticket.
* Notify appropriate stakeholders.
* Record escalation reason in audit log.

---

# 9. Agent Guidance

AI agents processing refund-related conversations should:

* Evaluate refund eligibility first.
* Review prior conversation history.
* Check customer status and account value.
* Consider retention options before recommending refunds.
* Escalate legal or regulatory matters immediately.
* Prioritize customer retention while remaining compliant with policy.

When confidence is low, route the case for human review.
