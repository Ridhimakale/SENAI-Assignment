# Pricing Policy & Billing Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines subscription tiers, discount eligibility, billing policies, upgrade and downgrade rules, cancellation procedures, and enterprise pricing guidelines.

Support representatives and AI agents should use this document when responding to pricing, billing, discount, upgrade, and renewal inquiries.

---

# 1. Subscription Plans

The platform offers three primary subscription tiers.

## Starter Plan

Designed for individuals and small teams.

Includes:

* Core platform functionality
* Community support
* Standard usage limits

Not eligible for enterprise features.

---

## Standard Plan

Designed for growing organizations.

Includes:

* Advanced platform capabilities
* Priority support
* Increased usage limits
* Team collaboration features

Eligible for approved discount programs.

---

## Enterprise Plan

Designed for large organizations.

Includes:

* Dedicated account management
* Custom onboarding
* Enhanced compliance options
* Advanced security controls
* Enterprise support commitments

Pricing is customized based on business requirements.

---

# 2. Non-Profit Discount Program

Qualified non-profit organizations are eligible for:

**30% discount on the Standard Plan**

Eligibility requirements:

* Registered non-profit organization
* Verification documentation provided
* Active organizational email domain

Discounts are reviewed annually.

Enterprise plans are not automatically eligible for the standard non-profit discount.

Custom enterprise pricing may be negotiated separately.

---

# 3. Academic & Educational Pricing

Eligible educational institutions may receive:

**20% discount on Standard Plan subscriptions**

Examples include:

* Universities
* Colleges
* Accredited schools
* Educational research institutions

Verification may be required.

---

# 4. Enterprise Pricing

Enterprise pricing is customized.

Factors considered include:

* Number of users
* Expected platform usage
* Compliance requirements
* Data residency requirements
* Contract duration
* Support requirements

Enterprise pricing is managed by the sales and account management teams.

Support representatives may not negotiate enterprise pricing directly.

---

# 5. Upgrade Policy

Customers may upgrade their subscription at any time.

When an upgrade occurs:

* Access to upgraded features is provided immediately.
* Billing adjustments are calculated using pro-rata billing.
* Existing service remains uninterrupted.

Examples:

Standard -> Enterprise

Starter -> Standard

Starter -> Enterprise

---

# 6. Pro-Rata Billing Rules

When a customer upgrades during an active billing cycle:

1. The unused portion of the current plan is credited.
2. The new plan cost is calculated for the remaining billing period.
3. The customer is charged only for the difference.

Example:

Customer upgrades halfway through the month:

* Remaining value of current subscription is credited.
* New plan cost is calculated for remaining days.
* Final invoice reflects the adjusted difference.

This process is referred to as **pro-rata billing**.

For customers with an existing approved nonprofit discount on the Standard Plan, support must account for that prior discount when explaining upgrade billing or pro-rata invoice differences.

---

# 7. Downgrade Policy

Customers may request a downgrade at any time.

Downgrades take effect:

* At the next billing cycle by default.

Immediate downgrades may require manual review.

Downgrades do not generate refunds for previously consumed services.

Support representatives should review feature compatibility before approving downgrades.

---

# 8. Cancellation Policy

Customers may cancel subscriptions at any time.

Cancellation results in:

* Access through the end of the current billing period.
* No future recurring charges.
* Retention of account data according to company data retention policies.

Refund eligibility is governed by the Refund Policy.

Cancellation does not automatically qualify for a refund.

---

# 9. Renewal Policy

Subscriptions renew automatically unless cancelled.

Enterprise renewals should be reviewed by account management teams.

Indicators requiring renewal review include:

* Customer dissatisfaction
* Repeated support escalations
* Churn-risk indicators
* Contract negotiation requests

---

# 10. Billing Disputes

Common billing disputes include:

* Unexpected charges
* Upgrade billing questions
* Renewal confusion
* Discount eligibility disputes

Required actions:

1. Review account history.
2. Verify subscription changes.
3. Verify applied discounts.
4. Review pro-rata calculations.

Escalate unresolved billing disputes to Support Leadership.

---

# 11. Discount Approval Rules

Support representatives may:

* Verify eligibility requirements.
* Explain available discount programs.

Support representatives may not:

* Create unauthorized discounts.
* Modify enterprise pricing.
* Promise future pricing exceptions.

Non-standard pricing requests require management review.

---

# 12. Agent Guidance

AI agents handling pricing-related requests should:

1. Identify customer subscription tier.
2. Determine whether discount programs apply.
3. Check upgrade or downgrade history.
4. Apply pro-rata billing rules when relevant.
5. Review enterprise status before pricing recommendations.
6. Escalate non-standard pricing requests.
7. Route uncertain cases for human review.

Agents must not invent pricing, discounts, or contractual commitments not defined in this policy.
